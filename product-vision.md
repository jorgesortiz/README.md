# Product Vision — SpendingHabits.io

**Author:** Jorge S. Ortiz  
**Last updated:** Q1 2025  

---

## Vision statement

A world where anyone — regardless of income or financial literacy — 
has a clear, honest picture of their spending and the tools to 
improve it.

---

## Mission

To help everyday people build healthier financial habits through 
simple, personalized, and non-judgmental insights — and to give 
credit unions a modern tool to deliver that value to their members.

---

## The problem

### For individual consumers
Most Americans know they should budget. Most don't — not because 
they lack discipline, but because existing tools fail them.

**The core issues:**
- Traditional budgeting apps are complex and built for finance 
  enthusiasts, not everyday users
- Manual tracking is tedious and unsustainable
- Generic advice doesn't reflect individual spending patterns
- Users feel judged, overwhelmed, and quit within days of signing up

**The result:** People remain disconnected from their spending, 
bills creep up unnoticed, and financial stress compounds over time.

### For credit unions
Credit unions exist to serve their members' financial wellbeing — 
but most lack the technology to deliver modern financial wellness 
tools at scale.

**The core issues:**
- Building proprietary budgeting tools is expensive and slow
- Off-the-shelf tools aren't designed for the credit union 
  relationship model
- Members increasingly expect the digital experience of big banks 
  without sacrificing the personal touch of a credit union

**The result:** Credit unions lose member engagement and struggle 
to compete with fintech apps that own the financial wellness space.

---

## The solution

SpendingHabits is a personal finance platform that:

1. **Syncs automatically** with bank accounts and credit cards — 
   no manual entry
2. **Visualizes spending patterns** in a clear, friendly interface 
   that doesn't require financial expertise to understand
3. **Delivers personalized insights** tailored to each user's 
   actual behavior — not generic budgeting rules
4. **Alerts users proactively** when bills change, spending drifts, 
   or budgets are at risk
5. **Scores financial habits** so users have a simple, trackable 
   measure of progress
6. **Partners with credit unions** to offer members a branded 
   financial wellness experience

## Who we serve

### Primary — The Everyday Consumer (B2C)

Someone who earns a steady income, has bills and subscriptions 
they've lost track of, and wants to feel more in control of their 
money — but finds traditional budgeting tools overwhelming or 
boring.

**They don't want a finance lesson. They want clarity.**

### Secondary — Credit Unions (B2B2C)

Community-focused financial institutions that want to deepen member 
relationships by offering a modern, branded financial wellness tool 
without building it themselves.

**They don't want to become a fintech. They want to serve 
their members better.**

---

## Strategic bets

### Bet 1 — Simplicity wins
The personal finance space is crowded but complex. Our 
differentiation is radical simplicity — if a user needs a tutorial 
to understand their dashboard, we've already lost them.

### Bet 2 — Alerts drive retention
Passive dashboards get abandoned. Proactive, timely alerts — 
"your Netflix bill went up $3" or "you've spent 80% of your 
dining budget" — create the habit loop that keeps users engaged.

### Bet 3 — Credit unions are an underserved distribution channel
Big fintechs target individual consumers directly. Credit unions 
have millions of members and deep trust — but no modern financial 
wellness product to offer them. We can win this channel.

### Bet 4 — Security is a feature, not a footnote
In personal finance, trust is everything. We lead with security 
— AES-256, TLS 1.3, SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001 — because it is 
a genuine differentiator with both consumers and credit union 
partners.
