## How we use OKRs

OKRs keep the team honest about what success actually looks like. 
Every objective answers "what do we want to achieve and why does 
it matter?" Every key result answers "how will we know we got 
there?"

> Rule: If we can't measure it, it's not a key result — it's a wish.

---

## Objective 1 — Build a product people actually stick with

> Deliver a financial habits experience so simple and valuable that 
> users return weekly without being prompted.

**Why this matters:**  
Retention is the only metric that proves product-market fit. 
Features don't matter if users stop showing up. Our primary 
persona — Brianna — has quit every budgeting app she's tried 
within two weeks. Winning her long-term engagement is the 
hardest and most important thing we can do in Year 1.

---

### Key Result 1.1 — Hit 1,000 monthly active users by end of Q3

- **Target:** 1,000 MAU
- **Baseline:** 0 at launch
- **Measurement:** Monthly active users (logged in + performed 
  at least one action in the last 30 days)
- **Owner:** Product / Growth

**Initiatives:**
- [ ] Launch B2C onboarding flow with bank sync in under 3 minutes
- [ ] Run paid social campaign targeting 25–40 year old consumers
- [ ] Launch referral program in Q2 to accelerate organic growth

---

### Key Result 1.2 — Achieve 40% Day-30 retention

- **Target:** 40% of new users still active 30 days after signup
- **Baseline:** Industry average for finance apps: ~25%
- **Measurement:** Cohort retention analysis — % of users from 
  signup week still active on Day 30
- **Owner:** Product / Engineering

**Initiatives:**
- [ ] Build and ship proactive bill change alerts (top retention driver)
- [ ] Implement push notification system with personalized triggers
- [ ] Run weekly retention cohort reviews — kill what isn't working fast

---

### Key Result 1.3 — Achieve average session frequency of 3+ times per week

- **Target:** 3 sessions per week per active user
- **Baseline:** Not measured pre-launch
- **Measurement:** Average weekly sessions per MAU
- **Owner:** Product

**Initiatives:**
- [ ] Design alert system to drive re-engagement (bill alerts, 
  budget check-ins, weekly spending summary)
- [ ] Build habit score that updates weekly — gives users a reason 
  to return
- [ ] A/B test notification copy and timing to maximize open rates

## Objective 2 — Launch and validate the credit union channel

> Sign and activate at least 2 credit union partners and prove 
> the B2B2C model works.

**Why this matters:**  
Direct B2C growth is expensive. Credit unions give us access to 
millions of members through a single partnership deal — and they 
come with built-in member trust. Validating this channel in Year 1 
unlocks a scalable, lower-CAC growth path for Year 2.

---

### Key Result 2.1 — Sign 2 credit union partnership agreements by end of Q4

- **Target:** 2 signed contracts
- **Baseline:** 0 at start of year
- **Measurement:** Executed partnership agreements
- **Owner:** Product / Business Development

**Initiatives:**
- [ ] Build white-label capability and co-branding documentation
- [ ] Develop compliance package (SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001 certs, 
  security overview deck)
- [ ] Present at 1 credit union industry conference in H1
- [ ] Build credit union admin dashboard for member engagement reporting

---

### Key Result 2.2 — Achieve 15% member adoption rate within 90 days of 
credit union launch

- **Target:** 15% of credit union's eligible members activate 
  the platform
- **Baseline:** No prior benchmark — establish with first partner
- **Measurement:** Activated accounts / total eligible members 
  per partner
- **Owner:** Product / Partner Success

**Initiatives:**
- [ ] Build seamless SSO integration with credit union account 
  credentials
- [ ] Co-develop member launch campaign with credit union marketing team
- [ ] Create in-branch and email onboarding materials for credit unions

---

### Key Result 2.3 — Achieve partner NPS of 8+ at 6-month review

- **Target:** Net Promoter Score ≥ 8/10 from credit union 
  decision makers
- **Baseline:** Not measured — establish at 90-day check-in
- **Measurement:** Quarterly partner survey (VP-level respondents)
- **Owner:** Product / Partner Success

**Initiatives:**
- [ ] Schedule monthly check-ins with each credit union partner
- [ ] Deliver admin dashboard with member engagement metrics by 
  launch day
- [ ] Build dedicated partner support channel — no ticket queue

## Objective 3 — Ship fast and learn faster

> Establish a product development rhythm that ships meaningful 
> improvements every two weeks and uses data to make every 
> decision.

**Why this matters:**  
We are a startup operating in a competitive market. Speed of 
learning is our biggest advantage over established players. 
Every sprint is a chance to get closer to what users actually 
need — or further away. We choose closer.

---

### Key Result 3.1 — Ship a meaningful product update every 2 weeks

- **Target:** 26 releases in Year 1 with at least 1 user-facing 
  improvement per release
- **Baseline:** 0 — establishing cadence from scratch
- **Measurement:** Release log reviewed in sprint retro
- **Owner:** Engineering / Product

**Initiatives:**
- [ ] Establish 2-week sprint cadence with defined ceremonies
- [ ] Create release checklist — acceptance criteria, QA sign-off, 
  release notes
- [ ] Maintain a prioritized backlog reviewed weekly

---

### Key Result 3.2 — Run at least 4 A/B tests per quarter

- **Target:** 4 tests per quarter = 16 tests in Year 1
- **Baseline:** 0 — no testing infrastructure at start
- **Measurement:** Tests logged in experiment tracker with results 
  documented
- **Owner:** Product / Engineering

**Initiatives:**
- [ ] Implement A/B testing framework in Q1
- [ ] Test onboarding flow variations first — highest impact area
- [ ] Document every test result — wins and losses both teach us

---

### Key Result 3.3 — Achieve conversion rate of 20%+ from free to 
paid tier

- **Target:** 20% of active free users convert to paid within 
  60 days
- **Baseline:** 0 at launch
- **Measurement:** Paid conversions / active free users at Day 60
- **Owner:** Product / Growth

**Initiatives:**
- [ ] Define and build paywall features that drive genuine upgrade 
  motivation
- [ ] A/B test pricing page copy, pricing tiers, and CTA placement
- [ ] Implement in-app upgrade prompts triggered by high-engagement 
  moments

## What we are not optimizing for in Year 1

- **Revenue over retention** — we will not push monetization at 
  the expense of user trust and habit formation
- **Feature count over feature quality** — shipping more does not 
  mean shipping better
- **Credit union volume over credit union fit** — 2 great partners 
  beats 10 bad ones
